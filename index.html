Comprehensive Medical Contraindications Assessment

DERMATOLOGICAL CONDITIONS - ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS
Active Skin Malignancies:
  • Basal cell carcinoma (in treatment area)
  • Squamous cell carcinoma
  • Malignant melanoma
  • Atypical moles/dysplastic nevi
  • Cutaneous lymphoma
  • History of skin cancer in treatment area
Active Infections:
  • Bacterial folliculitis
  • Fungal infections (tinea)
  • Active herpes simplex (HSV)
  • Impetigo
  • Cellulitis
  • Recent infection (<2 weeks)
Inflammatory Skin Conditions:
  • Active psoriasis (in treatment area)
  • Active eczema/dermatitis
  • Lichen planus
  • Seborrheic dermatitis
  • Contact dermatitis
  • Hidradenitis suppurativa
AUTOIMMUNE & CONNECTIVE TISSUE DISORDERS
Autoimmune Conditions:
  • Systemic lupus erythematosus
  • Discoid lupus erythematosus
  • Sjögren's syndrome
  • Rheumatoid arthritis (active)
  • Dermatomyositis
  • Systemic sclerosis
Photosensitive Conditions:
  • Xeroderma pigmentosum
  • Porphyria cutanea tarda
  • Polymorphic light eruption
  • Solar urticaria
  • Chronic actinic dermatitis
Scarring Disorders:
  • Keloid scarring history
  • Hypertrophic scarring tendency
  • Poor wound healing history
  • Ehlers-Danlos syndrome
VASCULAR & CIRCULATORY CONDITIONS
Vascular Malformations:
  • Arteriovenous malformations
  • Port-wine stains
  • Hemangiomas
  • Spider veins
  • Venous lakes
Circulation Issues:
  • Severe peripheral arterial disease
  • Chronic venous insufficiency
  • Lymphedema
  • Raynaud's phenomenon
Bleeding Disorders:
  • Hemophilia
  • von Willebrand disease
  • Thrombocytopenia
  • Anticoagulant therapy
NEUROLOGICAL CONDITIONS
Seizure Disorders:
  • Photosensitive epilepsy
  • Uncontrolled epilepsy
  • Well-controlled epilepsy
  • Recent seizure activity (<6 months)
Sensory Disorders:
  • Diabetic neuropathy
  • Peripheral neuropathy
  • Decreased sensation in treatment area
IMPLANTS & DEVICES
Electronic Devices:
  • Pacemaker (within 30cm)
  • Implantable defibrillator
  • Insulin pump
  • Cochlear implant
Metal Implants:
  • Titanium implants
  • Surgical clips/pins
  • Dental implants
  • Ferromagnetic materials
Cosmetic Implants:
  • Silicone implants
  • Dermal fillers
  • Permanent makeup/tattoos
Medical Assessment Protocol
  1. Comprehensive Medical History: Review all current and past medical conditions
  2. Current Medications: Document all prescription and OTC medications
  3. Previous Treatments: Assess prior hair removal methods and reactions
  4. Skin Examination: Visual inspection of treatment areas
  5. Risk Assessment: Evaluate cumulative risk factors
  6. Specialist Consultation: Obtain clearance for complex conditions
  7. Documentation: Record all findings and decisions
  8. Informed Consent: Ensure patient understanding of risks
Assessment Guidelines:
Always perform patch testing for uncertain cases. Consider cumulative risk when multiple conditions are present. Document decision rationale for all medium and high-risk cases. Obtain specialist medical clearance when in doubt.

SKIN & HAIR ASSESSMENT FACTORS
Recent Sun Exposure:
  • Active sunburn
  • Recent tanning (within 4 weeks)
  • Self-tanning products (within 2 weeks)
  • Minimal sun exposure history
Hair Characteristics:
  • Blonde/white/grey hair
  • Very fine or downy hair
  • Coarse, dark hair
  • Recently plucked/waxed hair
Skin Conditions:
  • Hyperpigmentation in treatment area
  • Multiple moles/freckles
  • Dry or sensitive skin
  • Previous skin reactions
Assessment Protocol:
Assess both skin and hair type before treatment. Skin types IV-VI require modified laser parameters. Hair with low melanin content may not respond to treatment. Always perform patch testing for uncertain cases or skin types IV-VI.

Comprehensive Medication Review

HIGH RISK PHOTOSENSITIZING MEDICATIONS
Tetracycline Antibiotics:
  • Doxycycline
  • Minocycline
  • Tetracycline
  • Oxytetracycline
Discontinue: 4-6 weeks before treatment
Fluoroquinolones:
  • Ciprofloxacin
  • Levofloxacin
  • Ofloxacin
  • Norfloxacin
Discontinue: 2-3 weeks before treatment
Systemic Retinoids:
  • Isotretinoin (Roaccutane)
  • Acitretin
  • Alitretinoin
Discontinue: 6-12 months before treatment
ANTI-INFLAMMATORY & PAIN MEDICATIONS
NSAIDs:
  • Ibuprofen
  • Naproxen
  • Diclofenac
  • Piroxicam
Consider: Discontinue 48-72 hours before
Topical Treatments:
  • Tretinoin (Retin-A)
  • Adapalene
  • Benzoyl peroxide
  • Alpha hydroxy acids
Discontinue: 2-4 weeks in treatment area
CARDIOVASCULAR & DIURETIC MEDICATIONS
Thiazide Diuretics:
  • Hydrochlorothiazide
  • Chlorthalidone
  • Indapamide
Other Cardiovascular:
  • Amiodarone
  • ACE inhibitors
  • Beta blockers
HERBAL SUPPLEMENTS & NATURAL PRODUCTS
Photosensitizing Herbs:
  • St John's Wort
  • Dong Quai
  • Lime/Bergamot oil
  • Fennel supplements
Other Supplements:
  • High-dose Vitamin E
  • Beta-carotene
  • Fish oil (high doses)
Medication Assessment Protocol:
Always obtain complete medication history including OTC drugs and supplements. Never advise discontinuation of prescribed medications without prescriber consultation. Document all medications and photosensitivity risks assessed.

Hormonal Conditions & Hair Growth Disorders

HORMONAL DISORDERS AFFECTING HAIR GROWTH
Androgen-Related Conditions:
  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
  • Hirsutism
  • Congenital adrenal hyperplasia
  • Androgen therapy
Thyroid Disorders:
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Hyperthyroidism
  • Hashimoto's thyroiditis
Other Conditions:
  • Menopause/perimenopause
  • Hormonal contraceptive use
  • Cushing's syndrome
Hormonal Assessment Considerations:
Hormonal conditions may cause ongoing hair growth, requiring more treatments or maintenance sessions. Consider treating underlying hormonal imbalance before starting laser hair removal for optimal results.

Pregnancy & Breastfeeding Assessment

PREGNANCY STATUS - ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION
  • Confirmed pregnancy (any trimester)
  • Suspected pregnancy
  • Trying to conceive
  • Breastfeeding (relative contraindication)
Pregnancy Protocol:
Always verify pregnancy status before each treatment session. Pregnancy test may be required if menstrual period is late. Treatment should be postponed until after pregnancy and ideally after breastfeeding completion.

Anatomical Area Considerations

SPECIAL ANATOMICAL CONSIDERATIONS
Facial Areas:
  • Close to eyes (safety glasses required)
  • Upper lip
  • Chin and jawline
  • Around nose (careful positioning)
Genital Areas:
  • Bikini line
  • Brazilian area
  • Male genital area
  • Internal mucous membranes
Other Sensitive Areas:
  • Underarms
  • Legs and arms
  • Neck area
  • Areas with thin skin
Anatomical Assessment:
Different body areas require different laser parameters and safety precautions. Always use appropriate eye protection for facial treatments. Exercise extra caution around sensitive and intimate areas.

Lifestyle & Environmental Factors

LIFESTYLE ASSESSMENT
Sun Exposure:
  • Recent sunbathing
  • Planned sun holidays
  • Occupational sun exposure
  • Regular sunscreen use
Hair Removal History:
  • Recent waxing/plucking (<6 weeks)
  • Shaving only
  • Electrolysis history
  • Previous laser treatments
General Health Factors:
  • Smoking (impairs healing)
  • Poor nutrition
  • High stress levels
  • Inadequate sleep
Pre-Treatment Lifestyle Requirements:
  • No sun exposure 4-6 weeks before treatment
  • Stop waxing/plucking 6 weeks before treatment
  • Shave treatment area 24-48 hours before appointment
  • Discontinue self-tanning products 2 weeks prior
  • Stay well-hydrated before treatment
  • Avoid alcohol 24 hours before treatment

Post-Treatment Care Requirements

IMMEDIATE POST-TREATMENT CARE (0-24 HOURS)
  • Apply cold compress to reduce inflammation
  • Keep treatment area clean and dry
  • Apply prescribed soothing gel/cream
  • Avoid hot showers, baths, or saunas
  • No vigorous exercise or sweating
  • Avoid tight-fitting clothing over treated areas
  • No picking or scratching treated area
ONGOING AFTERCARE (1-6 WEEKS)
  • Strict sun avoidance for 6 weeks
  • Use SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
  • Gentle exfoliation after 1 week
  • Moisturize treated areas regularly
  • Shave only - no waxing or plucking
  • Avoid swimming pools/hot tubs for 48-72 hours
  • Monitor for signs of infection or adverse reactions
WARNING SIGNS - SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION
  • Signs of infection (pus, increasing redness, fever)
  • Severe or worsening pain after 48 hours
  • Unexpected blistering or crusting
  • Severe allergic reaction symptoms
  • Significant pigmentary changes
  • Scarring or textural changes

Informed Consent Requirements

Essential Consent Elements:
  • Nature of laser hair removal procedure explained
  • Expected treatment course and number of sessions
  • Potential risks and complications outlined
  • Realistic expectations regarding results
  • Alternative hair removal methods discussed
  • Cost structure and payment terms agreed
  • Pre and post-treatment instructions provided
  • Photography consent obtained if applicable
  • Right to withdraw consent acknowledged
  • Emergency contact procedures explained
Key Discussion Points for Informed Consent
  • Treatment Effectiveness: Results vary based on hair and skin type
  • Number of Sessions: Typically 6-8 treatments, may require maintenance
  • Discomfort Level: Mild to moderate discomfort expected
  • Potential Side Effects: Redness, swelling, pigmentation changes
  • Rare Complications: Burns, scarring, permanent pigmentation changes
  • Financial Commitment: Multiple sessions required for optimal results
  • Lifestyle Requirements: Sun avoidance and specific aftercare protocols
Documentation Requirements:
Maintain comprehensive records including medical history, assessment findings, treatment parameters, informed consent documentation, and progress notes. Ensure all documentation meets professional and regulatory standards.

Risk Classification & Assessment Legend

Medium Risk: Relative contraindication - Specialist consultation required
Low Risk: Caution advised - Monitor closely and modify parameters
